Ubaye Hypertelescope: shape of the main cable

The telescope gondola which is represented by the green rectangle is suspended to a main cable (yellow) attached to both sides of the valley. The figure shows the shape of the cable at rest position when only taking into account its own weight and the vertical tension of the gondola. This vertical tension, expressed in kilograms, represents the sum of the gondola weight and the combined tension of the 3 driving cables (not represented in the schema). The effect of wind is ignored.

For the Ubaye Hypertelescope, the main cable anchors are at 2240m of altitude. The gondola needs to be placed 101m above the ground at the center of the valley (altitude 2046m). The aim is then to adjust the parameters to reach approximately 101m of vertical distance above the valley minimum. The two anchor points are separeted by a distance of 784m.

The valley profiles accuracy is only a few meters. This page can be used to compute the order of magnitude for the distances and the tensions. Two profiles have been measured at the Moutiere site: the South-North transect passing through the current location of the C8 celestron telescope which is used to record the hypertelescope image. Another transect, 80m West, is the approximate location suited for the largest hypertelescope in the area. The last transect is located in the Spiti valley in the Himalayas around the point of coordinates 32.048679°N and 78.104725°W.

The numerical values can be changed using the sliders at the bottom of the page to explore how the cable shape changes, and what is the maximum height for the gondola.
